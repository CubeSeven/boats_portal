## What Is Tsougria?

Tsougria (also written Tsougkrias) is a small, uninhabited island located approximately 3 nautical miles south of Skiathos town. It has two sandy beaches, crystal-clear turquoise water, a small seasonal taverna (open June–August), and almost no development of any kind.

It's one of the most popular short boat trips from Skiathos — and for good reason.

## What to Do There

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit:

- **Main beach (Mega Amos):** 200m sandy beach, calm water, seasonal snack bar. Loungers for hire.
- **Micro Amos:** The smaller northern beach. Quieter, better snorkelling. Accessible by a 5-min path.
- **Snorkelling:** The rocks at the western tip have excellent marine life — sea urchins, octopus, small fish shoals.
- **Hiking:** A basic path crosses the entire island in 40 minutes. The views of Skiathos town from the high point are worth it.

## Best Time to Visit

Early June and September are ideal — less crowded, water still warm. In August, arrive before 9am or after 4pm to avoid the caique rush.

**Distance from Skiathos port:** ~3 nautical miles (20–25 min by motorboat)
