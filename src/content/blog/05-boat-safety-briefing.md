## The Short Answer: Yes, With Preparation

The Aegean Sea around Skiathos is generally safe for recreational boating in summer — but the Aegean is not a lake. The famous Meltemi wind can push conditions from flat calm to choppy in a matter of hours, especially in July and August.

This guide gives you the honest picture.

**Key facts:**
- Strongest between 12:00–18:00, typically calms by evening
- Beaufort 4–6 is common; above 5 we do not operate
- Always check [windy.com](https://windy.com) or [poseidon.hcmr.gr](https://poseidon.hcmr.gr) before departure

## Required Safety Equipment

Greek maritime law requires all rental boats to carry:
- Life jackets for every passenger (we provide all sizes)
- Fire extinguisher
- First aid kit
- Flare signals
- Anchor and rope
- VHF radio (on larger vessels)

We check and restock all equipment before every rental.

## Our Verdict for Beginners

If you're nervous, start with a no-license boat on a calm morning. Stick to the south coast. By lunchtime you'll be confident enough to explore further. We've never had a rental where a beginner wasn't completely comfortable within the first 30 minutes on the water.
