## Banana Beach at a Glance

Banana Beach (sometimes called Krassa Bay) is Skiathos's premier party and watersports destination. Located on the southwestern tip of the island, 13 km from Skiathos Town, it's a long sandy beach backed by pine forests with a vibrant, youthful atmosphere. If Koukounaries is the family favourite, Banana is the adrenaline alternative.

## Getting There by Land

Banana is the final stop on the island bus route — Stop 26 (same stop as Koukounaries, then a 5-minute walk down a path through the pine forest). The bus from Skiathos Town takes about 35 minutes. There is limited parking if you drive. The walk down is steep in parts but shaded.

## Why Arrive by Boat Instead

Banana faces directly southwest and is open to the Aegean. The bay is broad with a sandy bottom — easy anchoring. Arriving by boat means:

- **No crowded bus** in peak season
- **No parking stress**
- **Direct beach access** — your boat becomes your personal base with shade, drinks, and a place to retreat from the midday sun
- **Easy hop to Mikri Banana** (300m west by water, inaccessible by land)
- **Combine with Koukounaries and Agia Eleni** in a single day trip

### Anchoring
Drop anchor in 4–8 metres over sand, about 60–100 metres offshore. The bay can get a swell if the wind is from the south or southwest, but those winds are rare in summer. In the prevailing northerlies, Banana is well protected.

### Rental Boat Access
From Skiathos Port, a 30HP boat reaches Banana in 40–45 minutes following the south coast. You'll pass 10+ beaches along the way, making it an excellent day-trip route.

### Water Taxi
A water taxi from Skiathos Town takes roughly 25 minutes and costs approximately €40–55.

## The Beach Experience

### Atmosphere and Crowd
Banana attracts a younger, livelier crowd. The beach bars play music all day, ramping up into late afternoon parties. The southern section is more active with watersports; the central area has sunbeds and umbrellas; the northern end is slightly quieter.

### Facilities
- Sunbeds and umbrellas (€10–15 per set)
- Two beach bars with full food and drink menus
- Watersports centre offering jet skis, water skiing, wakeboarding, banana boat rides, paddleboards, and kayaks
- Volleyball net on the beach
- Showers

### Watersports at Banana
This is the place for watersports on Skiathos:
- **Jet ski rental:** 15–30 minute sessions, €40–80
- **Banana boat rides:** €15 per person
- **Water skiing and wakeboarding:** Lessons available, €30–50 per session
- **Stand-up paddleboards and kayaks:** €10–15 per hour

## Mikri Banana (Little Banana) — The Quieter Sister

Just 300 metres west, accessible by a coastal path or by boat, lies Mikri Banana (Little Banana). This smaller, more secluded beach is famously nudist-friendly and has a completely different atmosphere — quiet, relaxed, and naturist-oriented. One beach bar serves drinks and light food.

From your boat, you can anchor between the two and enjoy both worlds — the energy of Banana and the tranquility of Mikri Banana.

## Combining Your Banana Beach Boat Trip

- **Start at Banana** (arrive early for the best anchoring spot)
- **Hop to Mikri Banana** (5 minutes by boat or a short walk)
- **Koukounaries** (5 minutes east by boat, visible from Banana)
- **Agia Eleni** (3 minutes west by boat)
- **Stop for lunch** at a seaside taverna near Koukounaries

## Best Time to Visit

- **June and early July:** Warm water, lively atmosphere, but not overwhelmingly crowded.
- **Late July and August:** Peak party season. The beach is packed and noisy. If you want the party, this is the time. If you want peace, go early morning or late afternoon.
- **September:** Still warm, less crowded, but the bars may have reduced hours.
- **Weekdays:** Slightly quieter than weekends, though the difference is marginal in high season.
