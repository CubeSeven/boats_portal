## What We See People Forget (Every Single Day)

Running a boat rental operation, we see the same mistakes made hundreds of times per season. People bring too much, forget the essentials, and arrive back burnt, dehydrated, or wishing they'd packed a waterproof phone case.

This checklist fixes all of that.

## The Non-Negotiables

These are the things we see forgotten most often at the dock — don't be the person who shows up without them: — waterproof, and reapply every 2 hours
- **Sunglasses** — polarised for glare reduction on the water
- **Hat** — a wide-brim hat you don't mind getting wet
- **Water** — minimum 1.5L per person, more in August
- **Valid ID / passport copy** — required for rental documentation
- **Cash** — some tavernas and anchoring spots are cash only

## On the Water Essentials

- **Waterproof phone case** — non-negotiable near salt water
- **Light coverup / sarong** — for wind protection when underway
- **Reef-safe sunscreen** — protect Skiathos' marine life
- **Motion sickness bands** — for those prone to sea sickness in chop
- **Dry bag** — for phone, keys, wallet, camera

## Snacks & Food

You'll be hungry. We provide a cooler with ice on every rental, and most supermarkets near the port sell sealed snacks. Our recommendations: — simple, no-mess boat food
- **Fruit** — grapes and watermelon are refreshing on the water
- **Sealed snacks** — crisps, nuts, dried fruit
- **Ice cooler provided** — bring ice from a supermarket en route

## What NOT to Bring

- Flip-flops with no grip (deck shoes or bare feet are safer)
- Heavy electronics that can't get wet
- Glass bottles (we prohibit glass on board for safety)
- Excessive luggage — a day bag per person maximum
- Heavy books — your eyes will be on the water

## Photography Tips

Golden hour on the Aegean is unreal — and your phone camera will struggle to capture it properly. Bring a waterproof action camera or an older phone you're comfortable getting wet.
